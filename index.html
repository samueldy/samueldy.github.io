Welcome!

I am a third-year graduate student in the Goldsmith Computational Modeling Laboratory at the University of Michigan. My research centers on using computational catalysis and machine learning for environmental sciences, specifically water remediation. I am also a 2018–2019 Fellow of the Michigan Institute for Computational Discovery and Engineering and a member of the Michigan Catalysis Science and Technology Institute.

I completed my B.S. in Chemical Engineering at Cornell University (2016), where I worked for the John Marohn Group in analytical chemistry. Before coming to University of Michigan, I served for two years as a logistics and product engineer for the U.S. Army Combat Capabilities Development Command Chemical Biological Center.

In my free time, I enjoy swimming, music composition, and organ performance.
